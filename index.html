About

Most teenage projects die by month three.

Sometimes, whenever you start working on a project, things happen and you have to give up. Not for me, though. It's not about the greatness of the product, or how popular it is — it's about the experience of building it.

I've built websites that took me years to develop, not because they should have taken that long, but because I wanted to learn. When I started building SenseiSuite, I began with block coding, then slowly typing, before moving on to agentic coding tools — and now I have a fully functioning business.

It's not about the goal, or the end result, or whatever you're trying to reach for. It's more about the learning, the process, and what you create along the way.
